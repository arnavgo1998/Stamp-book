Cover theme: stamp collection specimens
Style: elegant minimal hybrid
Aspect ratio: 2.35:1 (cinematic)

Title text: the stamp book
Subtitle: ten stamps, ten stories

Visual composition:
- Main visual: A single postage stamp rendered as a delicate line drawing — perforated edges, faint engraving lines suggesting a classical profile (like the Penny Black's Queen Victoria) — placed slightly off-center to the left on an expansive cotton-rag paper surface. The stamp floats with a barely-there warm shadow beneath it, as if just placed on a drafting table.
- Layout: Stamp at left-third, title "the stamp book" in elegant lowercase serif (Instrument Serif style) at right-third. Subtitle "ten stamps, ten stories" in tiny monospaced type (9px feel) below the title. Generous negative space everywhere — the emptiness IS the design.
- Decorative elements: Two faint pencil construction lines crossing the composition (one horizontal at the stamp's center, one vertical between stamp and title) — nearly invisible graphite traces at 5-7% opacity. A single small dot in muted postal red (#b44233) near the bottom center, like a navigation indicator.

Color scheme:
- Primary: warm near-black #1a1816 (India ink) for the title
- Background: cotton rag paper #f4f1eb — warm, with visible grain/tooth texture
- Accent: postal red #b44233 — used ONCE, for the single dot
- Secondary: graphite tones — #6b6560 for the stamp line drawing, #d4cfc8 for construction lines

Style notes:
- The aesthetic is "naturalist's specimen table meets contemporary minimalism"
- Think Dieter Rams crossed with a 19th-century field notebook
- The stamp should look hand-drawn with a technical pen — not photographic, not rendered, but *drafted*
- Typography at extreme scale contrast: large serif title vs tiny monospace subtitle (8-10x ratio)
- Paper texture is critical — the surface should have visible grain, like actual cotton rag drawing paper
- No gradients, no gloss, no digital effects — everything should feel like ink on paper
- The composition should feel like it was carefully placed by someone who loves stamps — not centered perfectly, but optically balanced
- Negative space does the heavy lifting — the cover should breathe
- Overall impression: quiet confidence, restrained luxury, the kind of thing you'd find on the desk of someone who collects rare things
